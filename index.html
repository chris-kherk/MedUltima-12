How Does This Treatment Work?

The Science Behind Potenza

This treatment uses very fine needles to make tiny injuries in the skin. These tiny injuries start the body's natural healing process, which helps make more collagen and elastin. The treatment also uses radiofrequency energy to heat the deeper layers of the skin, which boosts collagen production and tightens the skin. This two-step method makes this treatment very effective for many skin problems.

Preparing for the Treatment

To achieve the best results from the treatment, it's important to prepare your skin properly. Here are some tips to help you get ready:

  • Avoid Sun Exposure: In the weeks leading up tp your treatment, avoid excessive sun exposure and tanning. Sunburned or tanned skin can increase the risk of complications during the procedure.
  • Discontinue Certain Skincare Products: Your skincare professional may advise you to stop using certain products, such as retinoids or exfoliants, a few days before your treatment to reduce the risk of irritation.
  • Stay Hydrated: Drink plenty of water in the days leading up to your treatment to keep your skin hydrated and healthy.
  • Inform Your Skincare Professional: Be sure to tell your skincare specialist about any medications you take or medical conditions you have. This will help them customize the treatment for you.

Aftercare and Recovery

After getting the treatment, taking good care of your skin is important to get the results and avoid problems. Here are some tips to follow for aftercare:

  • Keep Your Skin Clean: Clean your skin gently using a mild cleanser that won't cause irritation. Stay away from harsh or rough products that might make your skin feel uncomfortable.
  • Apply Soothing Products: Use gentle and moisturizing products like aloe vera gel or a mild moisturizer to soothe your skin and help it heal.
  • Avoid Sun Exposure: Keep your skin safe from the sun by using sunscreen with a high SPF and staying out of direct sunlight. This stops dark spots and other problems from happening.
  • Avoid Makeup: It's best to avoid wearing makeup for at least 24 hours after the treatment to allow your skin to breathe and heal.
  • Follow Your Skincare Professional’s Instructions: Your skincare professional will provide you with specific aftercare instructions based on your skin type and the extent of the treatment. Follow these instructions closely to achieve the best results.
